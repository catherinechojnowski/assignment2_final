Overall NYC Crime Decrease Visible in South Bronx

By Catherine Chojnowski
November 12, 2021

While 2020 was an unprecedented year , it was also unprecedented in regard to crime rates in New York City. Overall crime reached a record low of 95,552 reported incidents, according to a report by the NYPD.

In the South Bronx, a community in which crime rates are among the highest throughout New York City, this drop in crime was visible on a community level.

Rosa Pinero, President of the Tenant Association of the Robert E. Moore Houses, credits the overall drop in crime in her neighborhood to the incorporation of Neighborhood Coordination Officers into the community.

“We still have the crime rate, but it’s died down,” she said. “The presence of [NCO officers] has made it a bit better.”

A South Bronx resident of 57 years, Pinero explains that crime rates were much higher in her public housing complex until the implementation of NCOs into the Moore Houses approximately three years ago.
